Course Description and Structure

Structure: Lecture/Discussion/Workshop

Description: Focuses on academic writing. Provides instruction in drafting and revising essays that express ideas in Standard English, including reading critically, thinking logically, responding to texts, addressing specific audiences, researching and documenting sources. Includes review of grammar, mechanics and usage.

Course Competencies

  • Develop an appropriate and articulate thesis using adequate support, sound reasoning, and valid evidence.
  • Plan, draft, revise, proofread, and edit to produce well-written essays.
  • Write in Standard English appropriate to purpose and audience.
  • Respond in writing to college-level reading to demonstrate comprehension.
  • Use library search tools and document sources appropriately.

Workplace Ethics Agreement

Present and Punctual

An absence is an absence; faculty do not differentiate between “excused” and “un-excused.” If a student is absent for more than 15% (more than 5 days), the student will be withdrawn or failed.

Professional Conduct

Students must conduct themselves professionally. Disruptive behavior, offensive gestures, or misuse of technology will result in dismissal from class and potential disciplinary action.

AI Policy: The use of AI text-generation tools or chatbots constitutes academic dishonesty. Submitting AI-generated work as original is recognized as cheating.

Important Policies

Plagiarism: 1st offense = Zero on assignment. 2nd offense = "E" for the course. Safe-Assign will be used on Blackboard.

Withdrawal: Students may withdraw via self-service until midterm. Post-midterm requires instructor approval and is only for emergencies.

Inactivity: After two weeks of inactivity (not logging in or not completing work), students will not be able to pass the course.

Late Work: Not accepted without prior approval in extreme circumstances. Lowest homework grade is dropped.

Healthy at SKYCTC: The health and safety of our students is our highest priority.
