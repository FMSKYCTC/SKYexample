The Art of the Transition

Developing Logical Relationships Between Your Ideas

Why Transitions Matter

Hello there! As we dive into your writing process, it is essential to remember that transitions are more than just "filler" words. They form logical connections between the ideas presented in an essay or paragraph.

"Transitions give readers clues that reveal how you want them to think about (process, organize, or use) the topics presented."

Pro-Tip from the Professor

Think of transitions as the "glue" of your essay. If your peers say your writing feels "choppy" or "abrupt," you likely have strong ideas but haven't yet built the bridges between them.

Common Pitfall: Over-transitioning

Don't force a transition into every sentence! Subtle transitions aid understanding, while too many short-phrase transitions (like "For example," "In addition") make the writing feel robotic.
