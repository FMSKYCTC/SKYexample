The Assignment Goal

In this essay, you will summarize arguments from two "Viewpoints" articles from the Opposing Viewpoints Database. You must synthesize their agreements and disagreements before entering the conversation with your own academic argument.

Pro-Tip: Only use articles labeled "Viewpoint." "Topic Overviews" do not count! Ensure your topic is arguable and offers a solution or new perspective.
Common Pitfall: Avoid "Banned Topics" like Anti-LGBTQ+ rhetoric, paying college athletes, or theological arguments. These will not be approved.

Key Requirements Summary

The Basics +
  • Word Count: 1000 - 1500 words.
  • Format: MLA with Works Cited page.
  • Tone: Academic language; No personal pronouns (I, me, my).
Quotes & Structure +

Use Candy Corn Structure for body paragraphs. Direct quotes are required in comparison/contrast and response paragraphs, but prohibited in the intro, conclusion, and summary paragraphs.

The "Third Opinion" +

You aren't just summarizing. You must assert a third opinion—your unique contribution to the academic conversation—and identify a scenario where your opinion might not work in the conclusion.
